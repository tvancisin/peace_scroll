The geographic spread of Russian engagement as a third-party signatory of peace agreements reflects its permanent seat on the United Nations Security Council and its role as a regional power. Like other members of the Permanent Five, Russia participates in large international conferences and in UN Security Council resolutions that function as peace agreements. However, most of its focus – and where its activity has been over the last decade – relates to conflicts in its neighbourhood and a number of select locales, where Russia is acting both as a military partner and a peacemaker.
